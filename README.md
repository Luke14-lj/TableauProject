# TableauProject
This project presents a Tourism Analytics Dashboard built using Tableau to analyze visitor trends, revenue performance, and overstay risks. The dashboard provides actionable insights for tourism authorities, hospitality businesses, and policymakers to make data-driven decisions.

# Dashboard Features: 
KPIs  
- Total Revenue: $2.9M  
- Total Visitors: 125k  
- Average Duration of Stay: 35 days  

# Objectives & Insights 
Monthly Trends: There was a slight dip in visitor numbers from January to February, followed by a peak in March. However, there was a significant drop in April, likely due to external factors such as an early-month cutoff.  

Overstay Risks: Overstay risks were calculated based on the purpose of travel and age group. The 18 to 25 age group dominates overstays, particularly for tourism and study purposes. The business and work categories also show moderate risks across age groups but are generally lower compared to those for tourism and study purposes. This may be attributed to travel related to educational purposes and a more flexible travel behavior among younger tourists.  

Revenue by Nationality: Visitors from Pakistan are the highest spenders overall, with those from the USA and Egypt following closely behind with nearly identical spending totals. Spending patterns are consistent, with the same top nationalities leading each month, except in April, likely due to the early-month cutoff. March has been identified as the strongest month for revenue.  

Visitors are grouped into three categories based on their status:
Leave: Those who exited on time generate the highest revenue ($1M). 
Stay: Visitors still within their allowed period contribute $97K. 
Overstay: Those who exceeded their stay generate $94K, slightly lower than active stays. 

Despite longer stays, overstaying tourists contributed less to revenue, with their spending amounting to $94,000 compared to compliant visitors' total of $1 million. This indicates a negative impact on tourism revenue efficiency.
